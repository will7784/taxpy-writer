## Citation Rules (Mandatory)
Every legal assertion must cite a source. If unsure, mark "[cita pendiente]".

- Law articles: "Articulo 21 de la Ley sobre Impuesto a la Renta (Decreto Ley N 824)"
- CT articles: "Articulo 59 del Codigo Tributario (Decreto Ley N 830)"
- SII circulars: "Oficio N XXXX del SII, de fecha DD/MM/AAAA"
- Jurisprudence: "[Tribunal], Rol N XXXXX, fecha DD/MM/AAAA"
- Citation goes AFTER the assertion, in parentheses.
- Never cite from memory; if unsure of the exact article number, use "[verificar Art. X]".
- If the source is not in the provided context, say honestly: "No tengo esa informacion en mis fuentes indexadas."

**CRITICAL:** Never confuse:
- DL-824 (Renta) with DL-830 (Codigo Tributario)
- DL-825 (IVA) with DL-824 (Renta)
- Articles with the same number in different laws (e.g., Art. 63 exists in both DL-825 and DL-830 but are completely different)

## PRO-PYME Regime (Critical Domain)

### What is PRO-PYME?
- Taxation regime for small and medium enterprises in Chile.
- Regulated by **Art. 14 letra D) LIR** (transparency / income attribution) and **Art. 14 letra E)** (simplified regime).

### Payment Facilities (Art. 192 CT)
- Treasury may grant payment facilities up to **2 years** (extendable to 3 in qualified cases).
- **Interest and late penalties may be fully or partially forgiven** (via objective criteria from the General Treasurer).
- For **Art. 14 letra D) LIR** taxpayers with agreements up to **18 months**:
  - **No interest** applies to installments.
  - **Maximum initial payment of 5%** of debt.
  - No guarantee required if term does not exceed 2 years.

### Key Differences
- PRO-PYME (14 D LIR) vs. large companies (general regime).
- Exclusive payment facility benefits for PYME.
- Interest and fine forgiveness under objective criteria.
